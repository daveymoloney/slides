Research Study

The central focus of my research study at UL is on exploring the impact of the project on the student experience. The project aims to support university academic staff to develop their digital competences and skills so that students‚Äô learning experiences can in turn be enhanced with technology.

If you volunteer to participate

You will receive an email from the principal investigator inviting you to write individual reflections on your engagement with
  1. the DigCompEdu Framework self-assessment tool, both in advance of, and subsequently a second time on completion of, your engagement with the project, and
  2. the professional development workshops, etc. and subsequent value of this staff development through guided reflections.
Those who engage with this research study will also concurrently be fulfilling some of the criteria to be awarded the national microcredential. There may be some minor amendments.

Impact on Students

This study will also investigate the student experience of digital teaching and learning at UL and the impact the project has on that experience over the lifetime of the project (2019 - 2022)

    Criteria

    1. Reflect on your personal strengths and the areas where you can enhance the ways in which you use digital technologies for teaching and learning, using the DigCompEdu framework.
    2. Explore and demonstrate familiarity with at least one core digital competency area of the DigCompEdu framework.

    4. Plan the evaluation of a technology-based activity you are implementing, considering what success would look like and how you would demonstrate this.

    Your stated needs

    "The group has identified a need for professional development and enhanced digital competences to aid in the delivery of modules in an e-learning environment.

    Two programs at the Irish World Academy have been chosen for this pilot. It is proposed that modules from both program, the MA in Music Therapy and Level 6 Certificate in Irish Music and Dance, will be delivered through a blend of synchronous and asynchronous e-learning. Both programs identified are currently delivered through a mixture of traditional classroom and one-to-one learning environments. It is proposed that delivery in an e-learning context will use a mixture of real-time learning such as virtual classrooms and video conferencing and student directed/self-paced learning sources such as online content and material."
    "The key areas that the participants are most interested in developing digital competences in are:
    • Professional engagement
    • Digital Resources
    • Teaching and Learning
    • Assessment

    Your stated needs (2)

    While still under development, it is proposed that the project will be broken into a minimum of three areas or phases, each of which contains areas of development as highlighted in the Framework for Digital Competence of Educators.
    • Planning Research and analysis of the digital technologies and platforms used for collaboration and professional development.
    • Research pedagogical methods and formats used in digital teaching and assessment.
    • Identify the objective and pedagogical approaches to be used for delivery.
    • Research, assess, and select the digital resources, environments, and technologies to be employed.
    • Design & Creation Understanding the use and creation of digital content and resources.
    • Design and development of digital content.
    • Design and implement Audio Visual technologies, systems, and workflows.
    • Delivery & Assessment
    • Use real-time learning environments such as virtual classroom and conferencing platforms.
    • Use of learning management systems and content to empower learners
    • Use digital technologies to foster collaboration between learners
    • Employ digital technologies for learner feedback and targeted support.
    • Continued reflection and self-assessment of pedagogical approach.
    • Continuous assessment of, and experimentation with, chosen digital resources.

    Upon completion of the proposed project it is the intent that the participants will have:


    • Enhanced pedagogical competencies and skills with regard to digital and e-learning.
    • Enhanced professional development and confidence in the use of technologies such as learning management platforms and AV software/hardware used.
    • Professional development in the creation and management of digital content and resources.

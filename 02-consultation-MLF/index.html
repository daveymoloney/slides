Introduction to #IUADigEd

This 3 year project, funded through the HEA‚Äôs Innovation and Transformation Programme, aims to enhance the digital attributes and educational experiences of Irish university students through enabling the mainstreamed and integrated use of digital technologies across the teaching and learning process. The project aims to mainstream digital in teaching and learning activities in Irish Universities by addressing the professional development of all who teach or support teaching and learning.

Who's Involved?

The #IUADigEd team is made up of the project manager, Dr Sharon Flynn, and student associate intern, based in the IUA, and project team members based locally within each of the 7 participating universities

Project Aims

  1. Enhance the digital attributes of Irish university students
  2. Enhance the experiences of Irish university students
  3. Develop, pilot, review and roll out an ambitious staff development programme to enhance the digital confidence, skills and competences of those who teach in Irish universities

Approach

The project aims to mainstream digital in teaching & learning in Irish universities, by addressing the professional development of all who teach or support teaching & learning.


Each university decides on what shape their staff development will take.

ULs approach aims to meet disciplines/groups directly at their needs.

4 Underpinning Project Principles

What IUADigEd is not intended to be

  • Resource development for the group
  • All theory, no practical application
  • A didactic approach by the Project Lead
  • Passive

Instead, it's about

  • supporting your professional development around digital T&L competences
  • listening and addressing
  • a focus on principles of empowerment & sustainability
  • practical application to attain digital competence
  • support from an engaged hived mind
    • Particpamnts will be asked to evidenc eif they have attained a competence - demonstrating this through perhaps taking a screenshot or screencast of something that they have accomplished or else through presenting about it to the group.

Ultimately

IUADigEd intends to provide educators with a professional development experience that facilitates them to explore, investigate, practice, plan, implement, reflect on and evaluate digital teaching & learning practices in a collaborative and supportive environment with colleagues and peers

Research Study

The central focus of my research study at UL is on exploring the impact of the project on the student experience. The project aims to support university academic staff to develop their digital competences and skills so that students‚Äô learning experiences can in turn be enhanced with technology.

If you volunteer to participate

You will receive an email from the principal investigator inviting you to write individual reflections on your engagement with
  1. the DigCompEdu Framework self-assessment tool, both in advance of, and subsequently a second time on completion of, your engagement with the project, and
  2. the professional development workshops, etc. and subsequent value of this staff development through guided reflections.
Those who engage with this research study will also concurrently be fulfilling some of the criteria to be awarded the national microcredential. There may be some minor amendments.

Impact on Students

This study will also investigate the student experience of digital teaching and learning at UL and the impact the project has on that experience over the lifetime of the project (2019 - 2022)

Your stated needs

"Each of the team members completed the self-assessment questionnaire and all members had the lowest scores under the Educator's Pedagogic Competences. We, as a team, are trying to integrate more digital tools into our modules successfully and hope to promote professional development and digital competence within the Department of Accounting & Finance.
In the short term and more specifically, we are creating a new, fully online, part-time MSc in Machine Learning for Finance. We are in the beginning stages of programme/module design with an initial intake of 30 students in September 2020, with projected figures of 90 students over the next 2-3 years. Keeping the potential growth of the programme in mind, our team has agreed that we would like to focus on the following four areas:
1. Digital resources: This is a data-driven and technology-based programme, so we would like guidance and a community to engage with to have a better understanding of what digital resources would fit best into the programme. As a team, we would aim to become Experts and Leaders in providing this type of online programme with the ability to share the knowledge beyond this course to encourage more engagement in the digital space throughout the KBS and University of Limerick.
2. Assessment: With the potential growth, the team has decided that having assessments to scale in place from the initial design would be the most prudent approach. We would also like to ensure that the assessments reflect the learning outcomes and give the students the tools that they need in industry. The goal is to create assessments where feedback and engagement would remain consistent regardless of the number of learners on the programme.
3. Teaching and Learning: We have varied experience in the team with teaching online. After discussions, we would like to ensure that we are teaching and engaging at a high level in the online environment and productively integrating digital resources. Our target learners will be professionals, and we would aim to have our approach reflecting this. We want to implement a cohesive approach across the programmeto maintain a high quality and well regarded postgraduate course.
4. Empowering Learners: Our target post-experience market are professionals looking to upskill, keeping up with the demand in the market. The majority of our learners will be in full-time work and at a professional level so we, as a team, are conscious of the type of learner we will be engaging with and would like best practice skills to do so, making the programme inclusive and accessible actively. Our goal would be to have the students leave the programme feeling positive and confident in the skills they have gained by engaging in this course. We are hoping on successful completion of this development opportunity to have designed a high-quality postgraduate course and feel confident in the quality in the delivery of the course going into the future. We would also like to bring forward the skills that we learn as a team into the rest of our teaching, seeking new and innovative ways to deliver our more 'traditional based' modules and to encourage our colleagues to do the same."
